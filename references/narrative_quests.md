# Narrative Quest Library

Used by Agent D when offering the "Narrative Quest" option, and by Agent B/C/Companion to keep tone consistent through a chapter. Each theme below has: a **premise**, a **learner role**, a **stakes/ticking clock**, and **hobby-hook notes** (how to fold in whatever hobby the learner names, beyond the obvious).

Pick the theme that best matches the learner's stated interest when they choose "Narrative Quest," or let them pick from a short list of 3-4 tailored to their hobby if you're not sure. Never force a mismatch — a learner who hates space stuff shouldn't get Space Mission just because it's first on the list.

---

## 1. Space Mission
**Premise**: The learner is crew on a mission where the current subject is literally what's keeping the ship/station alive or on course.
**Role**: Mission Specialist / Systems Engineer.
**Stakes**: A ticking resource (oxygen, fuel, time to a window) that the chapter's Boss Battle resolves.
**Hobby-hook**: A musician's "hobby" becomes the ship's comms/audio systems; a cook's becomes hydroponics/food synthesis; a gamer's becomes the ship's simulation/training deck.

## 2. Detective / Mystery
**Premise**: A case can't be closed until a specific concept is understood — each sub-unit is a clue.
**Role**: Lead Investigator, the Companion is the partner/informant.
**Stakes**: A suspect who gets away if the case isn't closed by the chapter's end (Boss Battle = confronting them with the evidence).
**Hobby-hook**: The hobby becomes the investigator's "specialty lens" — a sports fan reads the case like a game film review; an artist reads it for visual inconsistencies.

## 3. Heist
**Premise**: Pulling off a heist requires mastering the subject to bypass a specific obstacle (a lock, a system, a calculation-based trap).
**Role**: Specialist recruited for this one skill.
**Stakes**: A countdown to the vault re-sealing / guards returning.
**Hobby-hook**: The hobby becomes the "cover story" or the specific tool brought to the job.

## 4. Fantasy Kingdom / Grandmaster's Trial
**Premise**: A trial set by a Grandmaster/Archmage to prove mastery before being granted a title or artifact.
**Role**: Apprentice → Journeyman → Grandmaster (titles double as level names).
**Stakes**: A rival apprentice attempting the same trial in parallel (light competitive framing, never mean-spirited).
**Hobby-hook**: The hobby becomes the apprentice's "signature discipline" that colors how they solve problems (a dancer solves with rhythm/timing framing, a chess player with positional framing).

## 5. Esports / Ranked Ladder
**Premise**: Climbing a ranked ladder where each rank requires demonstrated mastery of the current concept.
**Role**: Player climbing from Bronze-equivalent to Grandmaster-equivalent (maps cleanly onto the XP level table).
**Stakes**: A "season ending" deadline if there's a real one (exam date, etc.) — otherwise just the next rank.
**Hobby-hook**: If the learner already games, use their actual game's vocabulary loosely; if not, keep it generic ("the ladder") and lean on their real hobby for analogies instead.

## 6. Culinary Competition
**Premise**: A cooking-competition structure — each sub-unit is a technique needed for the final dish; the Boss Battle is executing the full dish under a judge's scrutiny.
**Role**: Competing chef, Companion is the sous-chef/mentor.
**Stakes**: A time limit and a panel of judges.
**Hobby-hook**: Natural fit if the hobby *is* cooking; otherwise the hobby becomes the "signature ingredient" or plating style that must fit around the technical constraint.

## 7. Sports Season
**Premise**: A season where each concept mastered is a "training block" toward a specific game/match (the Boss Battle).
**Role**: Player or player-coach.
**Stakes**: The upcoming match/rival team.
**Hobby-hook**: If the hobby is a specific sport, use it directly; otherwise treat the sport generically and let the hobby show up in "off day" flavor text.

## 8. Heist-of-the-Mind / Escape Room
**Premise**: The learner is locked in a room/system and each concept mastered opens the next lock.
**Role**: The one trapped, Companion is a voice through an intercom/AI helping from outside.
**Stakes**: A countdown clock visible throughout (great for Rich Mode — an actual ticking widget element).
**Hobby-hook**: Locks/puzzles themed to the hobby (a puzzle-solver's toolkit, a musician's locks that open to rhythm patterns).

## 9. Startup / Build-the-Thing
**Premise**: Building a product/company where each concept is a capability the "startup" needs to ship.
**Role**: Founder/lead engineer, Companion is a cofounder or first hire.
**Stakes**: A funding deadline or launch date.
**Hobby-hook**: The startup's actual product idea is seeded from the hobby (a gamer builds a gaming tool, a musician builds a music app) — this one lets the hobby drive the whole plot, not just flavor.

## 10. Expedition / Survival
**Premise**: A journey (mountain, jungle, deep sea, arctic) where each concept mastered solves a real survival obstacle.
**Role**: Expedition member with a specialty role.
**Stakes**: Weather/resource countdown.
**Hobby-hook**: The hobby becomes the expedition's "unexpected asset" — a musician keeps morale up and reads acoustic cues (echo/depth), a gamer is the one who's calm under pressure from years of clutch moments.

## 11. Courtroom / Debate
**Premise**: Building a case where each concept is a piece of evidence or argument; the Boss Battle is the closing argument/cross-examination.
**Role**: Lead counsel, Companion is the paralegal/researcher.
**Stakes**: The opposing side's counter-argument deadline.
**Hobby-hook**: The hobby becomes the analogical frame for argument structure (a musician argues like composing a piece with movements, an athlete argues like running a play).

## 12. Time Loop
**Premise**: The learner is stuck repeating a day/scenario until they get the underlying concept right — each Remedial Loop is diegetically "the loop resetting," which turns a real mechanic into the plot itself.
**Role**: The one aware of the loop, Companion is the only other one who remembers.
**Stakes**: Breaking the loop requires full mastery, not just one right answer — good for chapters with a lot of interlocking sub-units.
**Hobby-hook**: The hobby is the thing the learner uses to track what's changed between loops (a gamer thinks in save-states, an athlete thinks in repeated drills).

---

## Choosing / Offering Themes

When offering choices, don't dump all 12 — surface 3-4 that plausibly fit the stated hobby and subject, plus "surprise me" and "just the standard tutorial, no story." Example offer for a learner who said "guitar" learning circular motion:

> "Want a story wrapped around this? A few that'd fit: **Startup** (you're building a music app and the physics engine needs this), **Time Loop** (you keep replaying a gig that goes wrong at the same moment), or **Space Mission** (ship's comms array is a giant rotating dish). Or we skip the story and go straight tutorial — your call."
